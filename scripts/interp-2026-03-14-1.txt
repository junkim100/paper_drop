HOST2: OK I need you to unpack what "mediated by a single direction" means for people who don't think in linear algebra every day.
HOST1: Fair. So think of the residual stream as the model's internal workspace. HOST2: So it's basically a contrast method. Show the model bad stuff, show it good stuff, and see what's different inside.
HOST1: Right. And the key insight is that the difference isn't spread across hundreds of dimensions. It concentrates on one. It seems to be a general property of how safety fine-tuning works.
HOST2: OK but here's the obvious scary question. If refusal is just one direction you can subtract, doesn't that mean safety alignment is incredibly brittle?
